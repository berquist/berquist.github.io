About

My research interests primarily focus on electronic structure theory, and include:

  • Pedagogical implementations of important algorithms, particularly in the context of Psi4NumPy.
  • Using safe and strongly-typed yet highly-performant programming languages for algorithm implementations, such as Rust and Nim.
  • Applying automatic differentiation to molecular property calculations.

My primary open-source contributions are to cclib, a Python library for parsing computational quantum chemistry logfiles into a program-agnostic representation, making it easy to perform complex analyses.

In the past, I was a contributor on Chemistry Stack Exchange, and could often be found in either the main chat, coordinating Q&A editing, or talking about computational chemistry.
